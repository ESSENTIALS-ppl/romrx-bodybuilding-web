9. Health Data & FTC Breach Notification

ROMRxBodyBuilding collects individual joint range-of-motion measurements tied to named individuals. This data may qualify as personal health record information under the FTC's Health Breach Notification Rule (HBNR), as amended effective July 29, 2024. ROMRx LLC takes this seriously and operates in compliance with the HBNR.

Nature of Health Data Collected

The ROM assessments collected by ROMRxBodyBuilding measure the range of motion of specific joints (hip, shoulder, ankle, lumbar spine, cervical spine, thoracic spine) for individual users. These measurements, combined with the Position Readiness Score derived from them, constitute health-related information about each user's physical condition. While ROMRxBodyBuilding is not a HIPAA-covered entity and does not provide medical care, the FTC's updated HBNR applies to health and fitness apps that collect identifiable health information.

Breach Notification Procedures

In the event of any unauthorized acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of individually identifiable health data:

  • Affected users will be notified within 60 calendar days of discovery of the breach by email to the address on file;
  • For breaches affecting 500 or more users, the FTC will be notified simultaneously in accordance with the HBNR;
  • Notifications will include: (a) the name or description of any unauthorized recipients of the data; (b) the types of data involved; (c) a description of potential harm to affected individuals; (d) what ROMRx LLC is doing to investigate and address the breach; (e) steps users can take to protect themselves; and (f) at least two ways to contact ROMRx LLC (email and website);
  • All breach incidents will be documented and retained for a minimum of 5 years.

Third-Party Sharing and Authorization

As described in Section 7, your health data is shared with Supabase (for storage) and OpenAI (for ROMBot AI processing).
